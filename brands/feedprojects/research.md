# FEED (feedprojects.com) — research + tokens + image plan

## Brand
- **Name:** FEED ("Fashion That Gives Back"). US brand. Page: home. Language: **EN**.
- **Sells:** canvas/leather totes, crossbody/book bags, wallets & pouches. Hero
  styles: Market Tote ($75–$148), FEED 10 Bag ($28), FEED 1 ($78), Woman on a
  Mission Tote ($38), Crossbody Book Bag ($68), FEED x minnow ($75).
- **USP / mission:** "Good design. Greater purpose." Every purchase funds school
  meals. "We believe that food is a universal human right." Impact stat (REAL):
  **128,341,386 school meals given to date (and counting).** Each bag funds a set
  number of meals (FEED 10 = 10 meals).
- **ICP:** socially-conscious women ~25–55, gift-buyers + self-treaters; want
  beautiful, useful goods that mean something. Objection the page must answer:
  "is it actually well-made AND does the impact claim hold up?" → real photography,
  reviews, quantified impact (the meals counter), clear give-back mechanic.
- **Tone:** optimistic, purpose-driven, accessible. "Style that gives back."
- **Niche:** bags-accessories → reference `references/bags-accessories.md` (Parker
  Clay DNA), approved by Bruno 2026-06-15.

## Real design tokens (from live site)
- Heading font: **louize** (literary serif) → use **Petrona** (Google) — same warm
  transitional-serif personality.
- Body font: **goodsans** (geometric humanist sans) → use **Manrope** (Google).
- Colors: ink #000/#212121, bg #fff; **primary CTA = clay #bf7259** (their real
  btn-1); sale/coral #e9514b; product palette = navy, army green, sand, stone,
  taupe, cabana stripe. Buttons rectangular (radius 0). Serif heads sentence-case,
  sans body, uppercase tracked labels/buttons.
- Redesign = FEED's brand (clay CTA, earthy neutrals, serif+sans) ELEVATED with
  Parker Clay structure: warm-parchment palette, mission woven mid-page (editorial
  beat + army-green impact marquee), origin/impact subline on every product card.
